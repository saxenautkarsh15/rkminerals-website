    <h2>What it is ?</h2>
    <article>
        <p> Pyrophyllite is isomorphous with the common mineral Talc, but has aluminium in place of the magnesium. 
        lt is very similar to Talc, with very similar properties and habits. </p>
        <p> It is of metamorphic origin formed as a result of metamorphism of deposits containing. Kaoline, diosbore,
        quartz assemblage. </p>
        <p> We are engaged in supplying a diverse range of Pyrophyllite Powder that is a secondary mineral of composition
        Al2O3SiO2.H2O. lt is hydrated aluminum silicate. </p>
    </article>
   </section>
   <section>
    <h2>Specialites of Pyrophyllite </h2>
    <article>
        Pyrophyllite is harder in comparison to talc. lt does not flux when fired.  
    </article>
   </section>
   <section>
    <h2>Uses of Pyrophyllite </h2>
    <article>
        lt is used in the ceramic products, particularly refractory. Pyrophyllite is used for ceramics, in paints, 
        insecticides, and in the production of rubber. lt is also used as a minor ornamental stone for carvings and sculptures.
