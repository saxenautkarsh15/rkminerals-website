    <h2>What it is ?</h2>
    <article>
     Quartz is the second most abundant mineral in the Earth's crust, after feldspar. Chemically, Quartz is silica, or silicon 
	 dioxide, SiO2.Quartz is a constituent of granite and other felsic igneous rocks. It os very common in sedimentary rock such
	 as Sandstone & shale and is also present in variable amount as an accessory mineral.
    </article>
   </section>
   <section>
    <h2>Specialites of Quartz</h2>
    <article>
     It is suitable for white opaque transparent glaze. Inferior quality is suitable for ceramic body. Quartz is rather hard having
	 a hardness of 7 on Mohr's hardness scale, and has a glassy (viterous) luster. Quartz is physically and chemically resistant to 
	 weathering. Most of the quartz is contained in granite, a smaller amount in metamorphic rocks, and a small amount in sedimentary 
	 rocks. However the highest concentration of quartz can be found in sedimentary and metamorphicrocks as sand stone and quartzite 
	 as well as in alluvium and marine sand.  
    </article>
   </section>
   <section>
    <h2>Uses of Quartz</h2>
    <article>
     <p>It finds use in glass industry and ceramics industry</p>
     <p>The white sands typically found in river beds and on beaches are ussually composed mainly of Quartz.</p>
